Instructions

In this experiment, your task is to evaluate outcomes in the marble drop game.

Here is how the marble drop game works:

  • Players drop a marble through an opening in the box (see example below).
  • The marble game has three sections. In the top section, there is one peg. In the middle section, there are two pegs. In the bottom section, there are three pegs.
  • At each peg, the marble has a 50% chance to go left, and a 50% chance to go right.
  • The player wins if the marble lands in a red box.
  • Games differ in which of the boxes are red. This means that some games are easier to win than others.

In this example, the player won the game, because the marble ended up in one of the red boxes. The player would have also won the game if the marble had ended up in the other red box on the left.

The experiment has two phases.

Prediction Phase: In the prediction phase, we will show you several different marble drop games and ask you to say what the chances are of someone winning this game.

Judgment Phase: In the judgment phase, we will show you what actually happened for a number of different games. We will only show you situations in which players didn't end up winning the game. Your your job will be to tell us which of two sentences better describes what happened in each game.
